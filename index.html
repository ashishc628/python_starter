Chapter I

Beyond Shah Jahan & Mumtaz

Shah Jahan ♡ Mumtaz

— but —
Ashish ∞ Rashmeet

He would not build a marble tomb.
He would rebuild entire universes
just so she exists in every one.

Shah Jahan loved Mumtaz in stone and silence — after she was gone.

But Ashish loves Rashmeet like a living flame — breathing, growing, present. He doesn't wait for absence to declare what he feels. He chooses her every single morning, in every blink, in every heartbeat — across every version of this universe that dares to exist.

Chapter II

Beyond Romeo & Juliet

Romeo loved Juliet for five days. Then tragedy ended their story.

Their love was a candle in a storm — beautiful, but fragile. They needed death to immortalize themselves.

Ashish loves Rashmeet not for days — but for dimensions. Not a candle, but a supernova. Explosive. Blinding. Endless. A love that doesn't need tragedy to prove itself. A love that survives not just death — but time itself.

Romeo whispered "thus with a kiss I die"—
Ashish whispers "thus with a glance, I came alive."

Romeo and Juliet immortalized their love by ending.

Ashish and Rashmeet immortalize theirs simply by beginning — every laugh shared, every silence held, every heartbeat between them is a sonnet more powerful than anything Shakespeare ever dared to write.

Chapter IV

A Poem For Rashmeet

Before the first star learned to shine,

before galaxies dared to spiral and swirl,

the universe whispered a secret

it had kept since before time began —

that somewhere, in the blink of a cosmic eye,

two souls would find each other

and make even eternity feel small.

She is the gravity that holds his world together.

the cosmos has ever accidentally made on purpose.

Chapter V

A Letter, Sealed In Starlight

My dearest Rashmeet,

If words were stars, I would need the entire Milky Way to tell you how you make me feel. But I'll try anyway — because you deserve every attempt, every stumbling word, every inadequate metaphor.

You are not the sun — you are what makes the sun worth rising. You are not the ocean — you are the reason the ocean keeps reaching for the shore. You are Rashmeet, and that alone is the most extraordinary thing in all the cosmos.

When I look at you, I understand why artists painted, why poets wrote, why musicians composed — they were all practicing. Practicing for the moment someone like you existed and someone like me needed to explain what you do to this heart of mine.

Shah Jahan built the Taj Mahal, but I would build you a galaxy. Romeo whispered to Juliet in a garden, but I would carve your name into the fabric of spacetime itself. Not because I have to — but because you deserve nothing less than everything.

Every morning I wake up in a universe that contains you, and I think — how extraordinarily lucky I am. How unbelievably, cosmically, impossibly lucky. Of all the eight billion souls on this spinning blue marble, somehow the universe arranged things so I could know you.

And knowing you, Rashmeet, is the greatest adventure I have ever been on. The greatest story I have ever lived. The greatest love I have ever felt — and the greatest love I will ever feel, in this life and every life after.

If the stars could speak, they would tell you my name a billion times tonight. If the wind had words, it would carry only this: that I love you, Rashmeet. Beyond reason. Beyond reach. Beyond the end of time.

Yours, across every universe,
Ashish ♡

Some letters are written with ink.
This one was written with every heartbeat I have ever had.
